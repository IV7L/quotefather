Fasil

Fasil is an automated web platform for structured debates, designed to restore clarity, fairness, and accountability to collective discussion.

Built on an adapted web-based implementation of Robert’s Rules of Order, Fasil transforms unstructured arguments into guided dialogue that leads to verifiable decisions rather than endless disagreement.

By combining procedural debate frameworks with AI mentorship, Fasil creates an environment where ideas are evaluated on reasoning, not volume or influence.

The Idea

Fasil exists to address the intellectual chaos of modern discussion platforms by introducing structure at every stage of dialogue:

  • Organize discussions into formal motions, objections, timed responses, and transparent voting
  • Guide participants using AI to manage turns, detect logical fallacies, and improve argument quality
  • Enable communities, institutions, and teams to reach decisions through structured, explainable processes
  • Preserve debate outcomes and voting results through verifiable, tamper-resistant mechanisms

Rather than amplifying opinions, Fasil is designed to separate strong ideas from weak ones.

Status

The platform is currently evolving as a prototype focused on refining debate flow, AI guidance, and decision transparency for real-world use cases across education, civic dialogue, and institutional governance.
